Missing Features — Full Priority List
Tier 1 — Critical, Ship These (High Need, Achievable)
1. Interactive Radar Map

The single biggest source of negative reviews across every weather app is inaccurate forecasts — users saying "said sunny, rained all day" — and radar is the primary tool people use to verify a forecast themselves. Every major app has it. WeatherWise doesn't. Use the free Open-Meteo radar tiles or RainViewer's free API for animated precipitation radar. This is the single biggest functional gap in the app right now. Unstar
2. Precipitation Timeline ("Next Hour" View)

Dark Sky built its entire identity on "Rain starting in 23 minutes." Apple killed Dark Sky and users have never forgiven them for losing this. Reviewers who loved Dark Sky's minute-by-minute precipitation tracking never forgave Apple — the replacement has minute-by-minute notifications in fewer regions, no rain-intensity graph. Tomorrow.io's API already gives you probabilistic minute-by-minute precip data. Surface it as a simple bar chart showing the next 60 minutes. This is a genuine gap in the market right now. Unstar
3. Multi-City / Saved Locations

Right now users can only search one city at a time. Anyone who travels, has family elsewhere, or plans trips needs to save multiple locations and switch between them instantly. Location and widget bugs including "widget shows wrong city" and "lost my saved locations after update" are a top complaint category. Store saved cities in localStorage, show them as tabs or a dropdown. One afternoon of work, massive quality-of-life improvement. Unstar
4. PWA — Installable App

Without this, WeatherWise is a website. With it, it's an app icon on your home screen, works offline for last cached data, and gets a full-screen experience. Weather is a daily-check habit — users need it to feel like an app, not a URL they have to find in their browser.
5. Historical Comparison ("Yesterday / Last Year")

The Weather Network shows this. "Yesterday's high was 18°C. Average for this date is 21°C." Users love context — it makes the forecast feel grounded. Open-Meteo's historical API gives you this for free. One API call, simple display.

Tier 2 — Strong Differentiators (Medium Effort, High Value)
6. Weekend / Event Planner View

A dedicated "Weekend" tab showing Saturday and Sunday side by side with hour-by-hour confidence scores. Tap any hour to see the full source breakdown for that specific time. The Weather Network has a Weekend tab — WeatherWise can do it better by showing inter-source agreement per hour.
7. Custom Notification Alerts

Right now notifications are only for rain and disputed forecasts. Users opt in to notifications expecting one type and receive another — notifications for rain in cities they don't live in, or cross-promotion abuse. Let users set their own thresholds: "Alert me when wind exceeds 30 km/h," "Notify me if tomorrow's high drops below 10°C," "Warn me if sources disagree by more than 4°C." This is the notification feature nobody has built well. Unstar
8. Feels-Like Prominently in Hero

Feels like temperature is often more useful than actual temperature. Right now it's buried in a stat card. It should be right next to the main temperature in the hero, styled smaller but always visible. This is a top user request across every weather app.
9. Moon Phase + Astronomical Data

The Weather Network has this. Surfers, gardeners, photographers, astronomers, hikers all care about moon phase, golden hour, and blue hour times. You already have sunrise/sunset — moon phase is one additional API field from Open-Meteo and takes an hour to display properly.
10. 14-Day Forecast (with heavy uncertainty disclosure)

The Weather Network shows 14 days. You show 7. Beyond day 7 your honest-forecast system is perfect for this — show days 8-14 as trend-only with maximum transparency. Your honesty framing makes this a feature rather than false precision.

Tier 3 — Polish and Retention (Lower Effort, Real Impact)
11. Daily Summary Email / Digest

"Good morning. Toronto today: 22°C, sources agree (87% confidence). Best time outside: 10am-noon." A simple opt-in daily email using your existing data. Nodemailer + a cron job. Keeps users engaged even when they don't open the app.
12. Commute Mode

Two configurable times (morning departure, evening return). Shows a compact card for each: conditions at that exact time, confidence score, and a one-line AI recommendation. "Your 8am commute: clear, 18°C, sources agree. No umbrella needed." Directly solves the #1 use case people open weather apps for.
13. "Was the AI Right?" Feedback Loop

After the AI answers a question, show a small thumbs up/down 24 hours later: "Yesterday the AI said bring an umbrella. Was it right?" Feed this back to improve the system prompt over time. Closes the loop on AI trust.
14. Keyboard Shortcut — Press / to Search

Power user feature. Press / anywhere in the app to jump to the search bar. Small touch, makes the app feel polished to technical users.
15. Dark / Light Mode Toggle

Currently appears to be dark-mode only. Some users want light mode for outdoor readability. System-preference detection plus a manual toggle.
